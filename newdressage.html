Riding Test

WHAT IS A RIDING TEST?

The dressage riding test differs from a normal dressage test as it looks at the Riders ability, position and effectiveness and not just how the horse goes. The feedback given should be constructive and positive to not only draw attention to the highlights but also show where improvements can be made to encourage better riding and higher marks. The judge can also give half marks.

Briitish Riding Clubs have tests at Prelim and Novice level. The tests can be downloaded by from the British Riding Club website. Test Downloads

TRAINING

We offer regular flatwork session including pole work and also arrange test riding events

WINTER QUALIFER

The Winter Qualifer usually takes place in February each year. Open to teams or individuals. The winter qualifer will be held on a surface

03/

SUMMER QUALIFER

The Summer Qualifer usually takes place in July each year. Open to teams or individuals and also includes Riding Test classes. The Summer Qualifier will be held on grass.

04/

PLAN THAT MOVE

Always make sure you plan your moves - a rushed transiton will cost you marks

05/

DRESS

Qualifiers and Championships abide by BD rules - make sure you are familiar with what is and isn't allowed to be worn for you and your horse.

06/

TACK CHECKS

There will be tack checks are qualifiers and championships. If you fail to get tack checked you could be disqualified. Make sure you are checked before you ride your test

07/

SPURS

Spurs are allowed but must not exceed 3cm measured from the boot not the spur itself
