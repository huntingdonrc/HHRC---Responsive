Combined Training

WHAT IS COMBINED TRAINING?

Despite its name Combined Training is actually a competition. It involves riding a dressage test followed by a show jumping round. The dressage test is frequently a Horse Trials dressage test. Your show jumping round must be completed shortly after the dressage test - you should have enough time to change tack if needed.

Seniors for teams of four with the best three scores to count and individuals. Two team members compete in the Prelim/75 section and two in the Novice/85 section. Juniors for teams of four with the best three scores to count and individuals. Two team members to compete in the Prelim/75 section and two in the Novice/85 section. Test Downloads

News and Tips

01/

TRAINING

We offer regular training and Cross Country clinics

02/

COMBINED TRAINING

This is a competiton that consists of a Dressage Test followed by a Show Jumping round. The scoring is based on BE scoring and the scores from both are added together to give a final total

03/

HUNTER TRIAL

HHRC run an annual Hunter Trial at Home Farm, Alconbury in October each year

04/

HATS

Riding hats must be to current standards. No fixed peaks for Cross Country

05/

DRESS

Qualifiers and Championships abide by BD rules - make sure you are familiar with what is and isn't allowed to be worn for you and your horse.

06/

TACK CHECKS

There will be tack checks are qualifiers and championships. If you fail to get tack checked you could be disqualified. Make sure you are checked before you ride your test

07/

SPURS

Spurs are allowed but must not exceed 3cm measured from the boot not the spur itself
